Overview

The task required predicting pain levels (no pain, low pain, high pain) from multi-sensor sequences. We focused on avoiding subject leakage, engineering stable features, and balancing model capacity with regularization to prevent overfitting.

Data pipeline

  • One-hot encoded categorical features and removed zero-variance / weakly correlated signals.
  • Applied PCA within joint groups to reduce redundancy.
  • Built sliding-window sequences (size 25, stride 2) with zero padding.

Modeling strategy

  • RNN/LSTM/GRU variants with dropout, label smoothing, and class-weighted loss.
  • AdamW optimizer and targeted grid search over architecture depth and hidden size.
  • 5-fold cross-validation ensemble with majority voting at inference.

Pipeline

End-to-end workflow from data profiling to ensemble inference.

1. Profiling + feature selection

Correlation analysis, variance filtering, and PCA to isolate informative joint signals.

2. Sequence generation

Sliding-window segmentation with stride 2 and fixed-length padding for each subject.

3. Model training

RNN/LSTM/GRU training with AdamW, label smoothing, and class-weighted loss.

4. Ensembling

Aggregate K-fold logits and use majority voting to produce final predictions.
